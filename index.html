About True Worshippers

Founded in 2020 by Apostle Solomon S. Adebowale and Reverend Mrs. Debby Adebowale, True Worshippers Evangelical Ministries International is a spiritual family dedicated to raising authentic worshipers who encounter God in spirit and truth.

We are called to equip believers for the work of ministry, build up the body of Christ, and carry the message of reconciliation to the ends of the earth. Through sound teaching, daily devotionals, and worship, we foster an atmosphere where lives are transformed and identity in Christ is established.

A JOURNEY TO THE HEART OF THE GOSPEL

Apostle Segun D. A. & Reverend Mrs. Debby F. A. Solomon
Hymn: “And Can It Be That I Should Gain?”
"The gospel is not a staircase to God but a declaration that He has come down the stairs to us."

Romans 1:16-17 & 1 Corinthians 15:3-5 (NKJV)

Romans 1:16-17: “For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ, for it is the power of God to salvation for everyone who believes, for the Jew first and also for the Greek. For in it the righteousness of God is revealed from faith to faith; as it is written, ‘The just shall live by faith.’”

1 Corinthians 15:3-5: “For I delivered to you first of all that which I also received: that Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, and that He was buried, and that He rose again the third day according to the Scriptures, and that He was seen by Cephas, then by the twelve.”

Devotional Message

The gospel is the good news that God has acted decisively in history to rescue sinners. At its heart is not a philosophy, a moral code, or a self-help program—it is a Person: Jesus Christ. Paul calls it the power of God for salvation, because it actually accomplishes what it promises. It is not advice; it is news.

To journey to the heart of the gospel is to encounter four realities: Christ died for our sins (substitution), He was buried (real death), He rose again (victory), and He was seen (eyewitness testimony). This is not myth but history. The resurrection validates that His death was acceptable to the Father and that He is Lord.

Why must we travel to this heart again and again? Because we tend to drift. We add human effort, religious rituals, or performance to grace. Paul rebuked the Galatians: “Having begun in the Spirit, are you now being made perfect by the flesh?” (Galatians 3:3). The gospel calls us back to the foundation: we are saved by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone.

Today, let the gospel wash over you afresh. You are not accepted because of your goodness; you are accepted because of Christ's goodness credited to you. Rest in that finished work. The journey to the heart of the gospel always ends at the cross—and then leads to an empty tomb.

Prophetic Prayer Points

Father, I thank You for the gospel—the power of God for my salvation.
Lord, help me never to be ashamed of the gospel, but to live in its power.
I receive afresh the truth that Christ died for my sins and rose again.
Father, keep me from adding human effort to Your finished work.
I declare that I am justified by faith and have peace with God through Jesus Christ.
Lord, let the gospel shape my identity, my security, and my purpose.
I renounce every attempt to earn Your favor; I rest in grace alone.
Father, fill me with boldness to share this good news with others.
I thank You that the righteousness of God is revealed in the gospel from faith to faith.
Thank You, Lord, for the living hope I have through the resurrection of Jesus Christ.

Word of Encouragement

Beloved, the gospel is not only the door through which you entered the kingdom; it is the path on which you walk every day. Whenever you stumble, look again at the cross—it shows you how much you are loved. Whenever you grow proud, look again at the cross—it humbles you. And whenever you fear death, look at the empty tomb—it guarantees your future. Journey to the heart of the gospel daily, and let it recalibrate your soul.

“For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ, for it is the power of God to salvation for everyone who believes.” — Romans 1:16
