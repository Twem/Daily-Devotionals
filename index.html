About True Worshippers

Founded in 2020 by Apostle Solomon S. Adebowale and Reverend Mrs. Debby Adebowale, True Worshippers Evangelical Ministries International is a spiritual family dedicated to raising authentic worshipers who encounter God in spirit and truth.

We are called to equip believers for the work of ministry, build up the body of Christ, and carry the message of reconciliation to the ends of the earth. Through sound teaching, daily devotionals, and worship, we foster an atmosphere where lives are transformed and identity in Christ is established.

Vision & Missions

Our divine mandate and core callings as a ministry

Vision

To raise a global family of authentic worshipers whose lives reflect the nature of the Father and who impact their world with the Gospel.

DISMANTLING DOUBT AND STEPPING INTO BOLD FAITH

Apostle Segun D. A. & Reverend Mrs. Debby F. A. Solomon
Hymn: “My Faith Looks Up to Thee”
"Doubt looks at the waves; faith looks at the One who walks on them. Fix your eyes on Jesus, and the storm will lose its power."

Matthew 14:28-31 & James 1:5-8 (NKJV)

Matthew 14:29-31: “So Peter came down out of the boat and walked on the water to go to Jesus. But when he saw that the wind was boisterous, he was afraid; and beginning to sink he cried out, saying, ‘Lord, save me!’ And immediately Jesus stretched out His hand and caught him, and said to him, ‘O you of little faith, why did you doubt?’”

James 1:6-8: “But let him ask in faith, with no doubting, for he who doubts is like a wave of the sea driven and tossed by the wind. For let not that man suppose that he will receive anything from the Lord; he is a double-minded man, unstable in all his ways.”

Devotional Message

Doubt is not the absence of faith; it is a battle within faith. Peter stepped out of the boat in faith—he actually walked on water! But when the wind and waves screamed for his attention, doubt crept in and he began to sink. Yet notice: Jesus did not let him drown. He reached out immediately and caught him. Doubt may cause us to stumble, but it does not have to be fatal.

James describes the doubter as a wave of the sea, driven and tossed by the wind. Such a person is double-minded, unstable. Doubt looks at circumstances, at impossibilities, at our own inadequacy. Faith looks at the character and promises of God. Abraham, we are told, “did not waver at the promise of God through unbelief, but was strengthened in faith, giving glory to God” (Romans 4:20).

How do we dismantle doubt? First, by speaking to it as Jesus spoke to the storm: “Peace, be still” (Mark 4:39). We command our souls to trust God. Second, by recalling God's faithfulness. David encouraged himself in the Lord (1 Samuel 30:6). He rehearsed past victories to build present faith. Third, by stepping out in action. Faith is not just belief; it is belief in motion. When you feel doubt, take one step of obedience, and you will find that God meets you there.

The father who brought his demon-possessed son to Jesus cried out, “Lord, I believe; help my unbelief!” (Mark 9:24). That honest prayer moved the heart of Jesus. You can bring your doubt to Him. He is not shocked by your struggle. He invites you to cast all your cares—including your doubts—upon Him, because He cares for you (1 Peter 5:7).

Today, identify the area where doubt has taken root. Bring it to the light. Speak the truth of God's Word over it. Then take a bold step of faith, trusting that He who began a good work in you will carry it to completion. The waves may still roar, but the One who walks on water is holding your hand.

Prophetic Prayer Points

Father, I bring my doubts to You; help my unbelief.
Lord, I fix my eyes on Jesus, the author and finisher of my faith, not on the waves around me.
I renounce double-mindedness and instability; I choose to trust in Your Word.
Father, strengthen my faith as I remember Your faithfulness in the past.
I speak peace to every storm of doubt in my heart; be still, in Jesus' name.
Lord, give me the courage to step out in faith, even when I cannot see the outcome.
I declare that I am not moved by what I see, but by what Your Word says.
Father, let Your perfect love cast out all fear, for fear brings torment.
I receive the grace to walk on water—to do the impossible through Christ.
Thank You, Lord, that when I sink, Your hand is always there to lift me up.

Word of Encouragement

Beloved, you are not alone in your battle with doubt. Every hero of faith had moments of questioning. The key is not to stay there. Bring your doubt to Jesus—He is not afraid of your questions. He is the Answer. As you look at Him, the waves will shrink, the wind will quiet, and your faith will rise. Step out today. The water may feel deep, but His hand is deeper still. You will not sink; you will walk.

“Immediately Jesus stretched out His hand and caught him.” — Matthew 14:31
