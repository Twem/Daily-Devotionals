About True Worshippers

Founded in 2020 by Apostle Solomon S. Adebowale and Reverend Mrs. Debby Adebowale, True Worshippers Evangelical Ministries International is a spiritual family dedicated to raising authentic worshipers who encounter God in spirit and truth.

We are called to equip believers for the work of ministry, build up the body of Christ, and carry the message of reconciliation to the ends of the earth. Through sound teaching, daily devotionals, and worship, we foster an atmosphere where lives are transformed and identity in Christ is established.

GROWING THROUGH THE PAINFUL TIMES

Apostle Segun D. A. & Reverend Mrs. Debby F. A. Solomon
Hymn: “It Is Well with My Soul”
"Pain is not a detour from God's plan; it is part of the path. In the pressing, the fragrance is released."

Romans 5:3-5 & James 1:2-4 (NKJV)

Romans 5:3-5: “And not only that, but we also glory in tribulations, knowing that tribulation produces perseverance; and perseverance, character; and character, hope. Now hope does not disappoint, because the love of God has been poured out in our hearts by the Holy Spirit who was given to us.”

James 1:2-4: “My brethren, count it all joy when you fall into various trials, knowing that the testing of your faith produces patience. But let patience have its perfect work, that you may be perfect and complete, lacking nothing.”

Devotional Message

No one welcomes pain, yet it is an unavoidable part of life. The question is not whether we will face painful times, but how we will respond to them. The Scriptures offer a radical perspective: we can actually rejoice in our sufferings, not because pain is pleasant, but because we know what it produces.

Paul writes that tribulation produces perseverance, and perseverance produces character, and character produces hope—a hope that does not disappoint. Pain, when embraced with faith, becomes a forge where our character is shaped. Just as pressure turns coal into diamonds, so trials, under the hand of God, transform our fragile faith into steadfast trust.

James echoes this: the testing of our faith produces patience, and when patience has its perfect work, we become complete, lacking nothing. The painful times are not meant to break us but to mature us. Joseph understood this. After years of slavery and imprisonment, he told his brothers, “You meant evil against me; but God meant it for good” (Genesis 50:20). What they intended for harm, God used to save many lives.

The writer of Hebrews reminds us that no discipline seems pleasant at the time, but painful—later on, however, it produces a harvest of righteousness and peace for those who have been trained by it (Hebrews 12:11). The “later on” is key. In the moment, we may not understand, but growth is happening beneath the surface. The roots go deeper, the branches stretch stronger.

If you are in a painful season today, take heart. God has not abandoned you. He is at work, refining you like silver. The process is uncomfortable, but the outcome is pure. Let Him do His deep work. Trust that He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion. The pain will not last forever, but the growth will.

Prophetic Prayer Points

Father, I thank You that You are with me in every painful time.
Lord, give me grace to see Your purpose in my trials.
I receive perseverance to endure and character to be formed.
Father, let hope arise in my heart that does not disappoint.
I declare that the testing of my faith produces patience.
Lord, make me complete, lacking nothing, through this process.
I trust that You are working all things for my good.
Father, let the roots of my faith go deep during this trial.
I receive the peaceable fruit of righteousness from Your discipline.
Thank You, Lord, for the harvest of growth that is coming.

Word of Encouragement

Beloved, you may feel pressed on every side, but you are not crushed. Perplexed, but not in despair. Persecuted, but not forsaken. Struck down, but not destroyed. The pain you feel today is producing something eternal. Let God complete His work in you. He is the Potter, and you are the clay—even when the wheel turns roughly, He is shaping a vessel of honor. Hold on. Growth is happening. The morning is coming.

“Weeping may endure for a night, but joy comes in the morning.” — Psalm 30:5

Apostle Solomon S. Adebowale
